The Research

IRL streaming is powerful.
The UX isn't keeping up.

Cloud OBS platforms solve the infrastructure problem, but the user experience — from first-time setup to mid-stream monitoring — creates friction that costs streamers viewers.

Complex First-Stream Setup

IRL streaming requires configuring SRT protocols, OBS scenes, ingest sources, and connection bonding. New streamers spend hours troubleshooting before their first live moment.

Source: IRL Streaming Community, OBS Forums

No Real-Time Health Dashboard

Streamers go IRL with no easy way to monitor connection quality, bitrate, CPU/GPU load, or viewer count from their phone. They're flying blind in the field.

Source: Streamer Community Research

Disconnected Clip Workflow

Creating clips, downloading, editing for social, and posting is a 4-tool process. Streamers lose highlights because the workflow has too much friction.

Source: Content Creator Interviews

Competitive Feature Parity

IRLToolkit, Streamrun, AntiScuff, and IRLKIT all offer similar infrastructure. The differentiator is UX — whoever makes it easiest wins the market.

Source: IRL Streaming Market Analysis

Mobile Monitoring Gap

Streamers are literally walking around. They need mobile-first stream monitoring, not a desktop dashboard they can't access while live in the field.

Source: Streamer Workflow Analysis

Social Content Afterthought

Streaming platforms don't help with the content lifecycle beyond live. Clips-to-social is left entirely to the streamer, losing the best moments to workflow fatigue.

Source: Creator Economy Research

The Solution

Meet Pulse

A unified command center that guides streamers from signup to first stream, monitors health in real-time, and turns live moments into social content — all in one interface.

Before & After

From fragmented to seamless

Without Pulse
4-tool workflow for clips to social — record, download, edit, upload separately
Manual health checking via desktop — no mobile monitoring while streaming IRL
Hours of setup configuring SRT, OBS scenes, and ingest sources before first stream
No content pipeline — live moments lost because posting requires too many steps
With Pulse
One-tap clip → social in the same interface with auto-formatted templates
Real-time mobile monitoring with alerts for bitrate drops and connection issues
5-minute guided wizard with auto-detection of connection quality and recommended settings
Social queue — live moments automatically saved and queued for posting after stream

Implementation

Roadmap

A phased approach designed for a 4-person startup — ship fast, get feedback from real streamers, iterate.

Phase 1

First-Stream Wizard

Design a guided setup flow that gets new streamers from signup to live in under 5 minutes, with auto-detection of connection quality and recommended settings.

Weeks 1–3
Phase 2

Pulse Dashboard

About the Designer

Maria Dumitrescu

I'm a design student passionate about creator tools and the live streaming ecosystem. Streamable Pulse represents my vision for how IRL streaming platforms can differentiate through UX — not just infrastructure. When every competitor offers 99.99% uptime, the product that wins is the one streamers actually love using.

This proposal is informed by research across IRL streaming communities, competitor analysis of IRLToolkit, Streamrun, and IRLKIT, and real streamer workflow analysis.
