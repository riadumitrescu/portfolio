Research Insights

The Visualization Challenge

Through analysis of IBM Research publications, design system audits, and industry benchmarks, I identified critical gaps in how breakthrough research is communicated visually.

Quantum Concepts Are Abstract

Bloch spheres only work for small systems (N≤5 qubits), and entanglement is impossible to visualize using classical analogies. As qubit counts grow, visual representations break down entirely.

5 max qubits visualized intuitively

Research-to-Public Gap

IBM Research spans quantum, AI, and semiconductors, but communicating breakthroughs to non-technical audiences remains a major challenge. Each domain has its own visual language with no shared framework.

3 major domains to communicate

Cross-Platform Inconsistency

Research visual assets are created ad-hoc across web, social media, conference presentations, events, and print publications without a unified visual system or shared asset library.

5+ touchpoints, no unified system

Static Explanations for Dynamic Concepts

Quantum circuits, AI model training, and chip fabrication are inherently dynamic processes, yet they are typically explained through static diagrams that fail to convey how these systems evolve.

Low engagement with technical content

Carbon Rigidity for Creative Work

Carbon Design System is optimized for enterprise dashboards and product interfaces, but offers less flexibility for creative research storytelling, editorial design, and immersive visual experiences.

Enterprise focused, not research-optimized

Talent Pipeline Challenge

Quantum computing needs 300K+ professionals by 2030, but visual communication of the field struggles to inspire and recruit new talent. The visual gap limits public understanding and career interest.

300K+ needed by 2030

Implementation Vision

Development Roadmap

A phased approach to building the IBM Research Spectrum system

1

Phase 1: Audit & Framework

Visual Language Foundation

Audit existing research visual assets across quantum, AI, and semiconductor teams. Map the abstraction framework linking technical depth to audience tiers. Define the visual grammar, color coding per research domain, and typographic hierarchy within IBM Design Language.

2

Phase 2: Component Library

Modular Visual Assets

Build a Figma and code component library of reusable visual elements: quantum circuit diagrams, qubit state representations, neural network architectures, and chip cross-section illustrations. All components follow Carbon grid and spacing, with domain-specific color tokens.

3

Phase 3: Interactive Prototypes

Explorable Explainers

Develop web-based interactive experiences using Three.js and Web Components. Create pilot explainers for quantum advantage, error correction, and AI foundation models. Test with researchers, communications team, and public audiences for comprehension and engagement.

4

Phase 4: Scale & Governance

System Adoption & Growth

Document the Spectrum system with usage guidelines and contribution workflows. Train research teams on the component library. Establish governance for visual consistency across IBM Research web properties, social media, conference assets, and publications.
