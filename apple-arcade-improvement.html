The Research

200+ great games.
Nobody can find them. 🔍

Apple Arcade has a world-class library — but discovery is broken. Games are buried in an App Store tab with no social signals, no mood-matching, and no reason to open it in the first place.

🗂️

Buried in a Tab

Arcade lives inside an App Store tab competing with everything else. There's no dedicated experience that creates intent — people need a reason to open that tab in the first place.

Source: Game Developer Magazine, MacRumors
🤷

No Mood Matching

"The lack of intelligent discovery doesn't help: Arcade doesn't always put titles that might suit your tastes front and center." No context-awareness for what players want right now.

Source: TechRadar Apple Arcade Review
👻

No Social Layer

Without the usual social signals, reviews, or community loops that push great games into your feed, gems stay hidden. There's no "3 friends played this week" moment.

Source: MacRumors, AppleInsider Analysis
📖

No Editorial Storytelling

Apple TV+ has cinematic trailers and editorial buzz. Arcade gets a thumbnail and a short description. The games deserve the same storytelling treatment as Apple's other media.

Source: Inverse Gaming Analysis
📉

Weak Retention Loop

Users come in, play one game, and forget to return. There's no dynamic homescreen that changes with new releases, seasonal content, or personalized picks to pull players back.

Source: MacObserver, AppleInsider
🤡

Crappy Filler Reputation

"There are some decent games buried in there but the UI/discoverability is pretty bad and there's lots of crappy filler content." The design doesn't surface the gems over the filler.

Source: MetaFilter, ResetEra Forum

The Solution

Meet Playground ✨

A mood-first discovery experience that opens with a feeling, not a catalogue. Tell Arcade how you're feeling and it builds your world for the evening.

Before & After

From buried to beloved 🏆

Without Playground
❌
Open App Store, find Arcade tab — zero intent, zero reason, competes with everything else on screen
❌
Alphabetical catalogue scroll — no mood matching, no context for what you want right now
❌
Static thumbnails, short blurbs — great games indistinguishable from filler without editorial storytelling
❌
No social layer — you have no idea what your friends are playing or what's trending among people like you
With Playground
✅
Opens with a mood prompt — "What's calling you?" builds intent before you see a single game title
✅
3 perfectly matched picks — surfaced by mood, time of day, play history, and friends activity
✅
Editorial story cards — human, cinematic storytelling that makes you feel the game before you tap
✅
Social-lite signals — "2 friends playing" and "trending now" surface gems through trust, not algorithms

Implementation

Roadmap 🗺️

A summer-length plan that ships from early concepts to polished prototypes — the exact cycle described in the role.

🎭
Phase 1

Mood Framework

Define the mood taxonomy (Chill, Thrill, Create, Explore), map the game library to each, and prototype the mood-selection interaction flow.

Weeks 1–3
✍️
Phase 2

Editorial Stories

Design the story card system — rich visual templates, editorial copy format, and how stories refresh weekly. Prototype for usability testing.

Weeks 4–6
👥
Phase 3

Social-Lite Signals

Design the lightweight social layer — friends activity, trending badges, and "played by people like you" nudges that create community without complexity.

Weeks 7–9
🚀
Phase 4

Polish & Present

Final high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, usability test findings, motion design polish, and the end-of-internship presentation to stakeholders.

Weeks 10–12

About the Designer

Maria Dumitrescu 👋

I'm a design student who genuinely loves games and the feelings they create. Playground came from a simple observation: Apple Arcade has some of the most beautifully crafted games on any platform — and almost nobody knows they exist. That's not a game problem. That's a design problem.

I'm excited about the full design process described in this role — from early concepts and storytelling to polished UI and prototyping. I want to work on products that make people feel something before they even press start.
